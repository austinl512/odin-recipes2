Tamago Egg

Description

Tamago (a.k.a. tamagoyaki) is a Japanese omelet. Itâ€™s made by rolling together thin layers of fried eggs, then slicing the log into rectangles. Since the egg is mixed with dashi, sugar, and mirin, the finished product has a complex and subtly sweet flavor. Tamago is traditionally served for breakfast or as a sushi ingredient. You can enjoy the homemade tamago right away or freeze it for up to two weeks. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator.
